Where to go

Main towns and villages

Each town has its own character, from market streets to seaside music sessions.

Ennis

The county town, known for shopping, traditional music, narrow streets and access to bus and rail services.

Doolin

A small village famous for traditional Irish music, ferries to the Aran Islands and proximity to the Cliffs of Moher.

Lahinch

A lively seaside town popular for surfing, beach walks, golf and summer holidays.

Killaloe

A historic riverside town on Lough Derg, linked with Brian Boru and outdoor activities on the Shannon.

Kilrush

A heritage town near the Shannon Estuary, Scattery Island and the Loop Head route.

Lisdoonvarna

A spa town near the Burren, well known for its matchmaking festival and traditional hospitality.

Past and culture

A county shaped by landscape and legend

Clare’s history includes prehistoric tombs in the Burren, early Christian monastic sites, medieval castles, Gaelic lordships, maritime communities and strong traditions of Irish music.

Visitors can explore places associated with Brian Boru, the High King of Ireland, as well as heritage sites such as Bunratty Castle, Scattery Island and the many archaeological remains scattered across the Burren.

Food, pubs and music

Restaurants and pubs

Clare is a great county for seafood, farm produce, cosy pubs and traditional music sessions.

Restaurant ideas

  • Look for seafood in coastal towns such as Doolin, Lahinch, Kilkee and Ballyvaughan.
  • Try cafés and restaurants in Ennis for relaxed lunches, local produce and evening meals.
  • Explore the Burren Food Trail for producers, cafés and food experiences.
Find food inspiration on Discover Ireland

Pubs and traditional music

  • Doolin is one of Ireland’s best-known villages for traditional music sessions.
  • Ennis has a strong music scene and many pubs within walking distance of the town centre.
  • Always check pub websites or social pages for current session times and opening hours.
